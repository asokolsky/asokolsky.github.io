# Dubrovnik to Trieste Road Trip: 5 Days

Assumption: rented car, one-way route, and no driving day over about 4 hours. This is a fast version. Rick Steves' public [Croatia](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Croatia)/[Slovenia](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Slovenia) guidance says short trips should focus on priorities and not underestimate distances, so this version keeps [Dubrovnik](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Dubrovnik%2C%20Croatia), [Mostar](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Mostar%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina), [Split](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Split%2C%20Croatia), [Zadar](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Zadar%2C%20Croatia), [Plitvice](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Plitvice%20Lakes%20National%20Park%2C%20Croatia), and [Trieste](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Trieste%2C%20Italy), while making [Brela](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Brela%2C%20Croatia)/[Baska Voda](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Baska%20Voda%2C%20Croatia) a coastal stop instead of an overnight. [1][2]

## Rick Steves Lens

- Prioritize the big-impact stops: [Dubrovnik](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Dubrovnik%2C%20Croatia), [Mostar](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Mostar%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina), [Split](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Split%2C%20Croatia), and [Plitvice](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Plitvice%20Lakes%20National%20Park%2C%20Croatia). [1][2][3]
- Use the car where it helps most: [Bosnia and Herzegovina](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina), [Peljesac](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Peljesac%2C%20Croatia)/[Ston](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Ston%2C%20Croatia), [Plitvice](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Plitvice%20Lakes%20National%20Park%2C%20Croatia), and the final [Slovenia](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Slovenia)/[Italy](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Italy) approach. [1][4]
- Keep old-town time unrushed: early mornings and evenings matter more than adding extra checklist stops. [1]
- Treat [Plitvice](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Plitvice%20Lakes%20National%20Park%2C%20Croatia) as the major inland nature detour. In 5 days, this means skipping [Opatija](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Opatija%2C%20Croatia), [Piran](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Piran%2C%20Slovenia), [Postojna Cave](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Postojna%20Cave%2C%20Slovenia), and [Skocjan Caves](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Skocjan%20Caves%2C%20Slovenia). [3][5]
- Skip [Hvar](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Hvar%2C%20Croatia)/[Korcula](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Korcula%2C%20Croatia), [Krka](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Krka%20National%20Park%2C%20Croatia), [Sibenik](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Sibenik%2C%20Croatia), and [Istrian hill towns](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Istria%2C%20Croatia). [1]

## Day 1: Dubrovnik, Croatia to Mostar, Bosnia and Herzegovina

Estimated driving: 3.25 to 3.75 hours total, plus border time.

Map: [Day 1 route: Dubrovnik -> Ston -> Pocitelj -> Mostar](https://www.google.com/maps/dir/?api=1&origin=Dubrovnik%2C%20Croatia&destination=Mostar%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina&travelmode=driving&waypoints=Ston%2C%20Croatia%7CPocitelj%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina)

- Leave [Dubrovnik](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Dubrovnik%2C%20Croatia) early.
- Stop in [Ston](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Ston%2C%20Croatia) and [Mali Ston](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Mali%20Ston%2C%20Croatia) for the walls, salt pans, and oysters.
- Continue to [Pocitelj](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Pocitelj%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina) for a short walk through the old stone village.
- Sleep in [Mostar](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Mostar%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina), ideally within walking distance of [Stari Most](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Stari%20Most%2C%20Mostar%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina).

Best stops:
- [Ston walls](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Walls%20of%20Ston%2C%20Croatia) and [Mali Ston](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Mali%20Ston%2C%20Croatia) oysters. [1][2]
- [Pocitelj](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Pocitelj%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina) fortress viewpoint. [1]
- [Mostar](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Mostar%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina) old town at sunset: [Stari Most](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Stari%20Most%2C%20Mostar%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina), bazaar lanes, and riverside views. [1]

Food notes:
- If you have a pre-trip evening in [Dubrovnik](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Dubrovnik%2C%20Croatia), [Buza Bar](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Buza%20Bar%2C%20Dubrovnik%2C%20Croatia) is the Rick Steves-published cliffside drink stop. For dinner, [Taj Mahal](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Taj%20Mahal%2C%20Dubrovnik%2C%20Croatia) is the Bosnian/Balkan pick with Michelin Bib Gourmand recognition, [Kamenice](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Kamenice%2C%20Dubrovnik%2C%20Croatia) is the simple old-town seafood/oyster option, and [Kopun](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Kopun%2C%20Dubrovnik%2C%20Croatia) is better for a composed Croatian meal near the Jesuit steps. [11][12][19][20][21]
- Around [Ston](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Ston%2C%20Croatia) / [Mali Ston](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Mali%20Ston%2C%20Croatia), make oysters the point: [Kapetanova Kuca](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Kapetanova%20Kuca%2C%20Mali%20Ston%2C%20Croatia) is the more established seafood stop, while [Bota Sare](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Bota%20Sare%2C%20Mali%20Ston%2C%20Croatia) is another classic Mali Ston oyster option. Keep this as lunch only if it does not endanger the border timing. [22][23]
- In [Mostar](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Mostar%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina), aim for Bosnian food near the bridge or Coppersmiths' Street. Good candidates are [Tima-Irma](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Tima-Irma%2C%20Mostar%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina) for grilled meats/cevapi, [Sadrvan](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Sadrvan%2C%20Mostar%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina) for a traditional courtyard meal, [Hindin Han](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Hindin%20Han%2C%20Mostar%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina) for riverside setting, and [Harmonija](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Harmonija%2C%20Mostar%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina) for bridge views. [14][24]

Skip [Kravica](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Kravica%20Waterfall%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina) unless the border crossing is unusually quick. [1][9]

## Day 2: Mostar, Bosnia and Herzegovina to Split / Trogir, Croatia via Brela, Croatia

Estimated driving: 3.5 to 4 hours total, plus border time. Skip [Imotski](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Imotski%2C%20Croatia) if the border is slow. [1][9]

Map: [Day 2 route: Mostar -> Blagaj -> Brela -> Split](https://www.google.com/maps/dir/?api=1&origin=Mostar%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina&destination=Split%2C%20Croatia&travelmode=driving&waypoints=Blagaj%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina%7CBrela%2C%20Croatia)

- See [Mostar](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Mostar%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina) early before the day-trip crowds.
- Detour to [Blagaj](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Blagaj%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina) for the [Buna spring](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Buna%20Spring%2C%20Blagaj%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina) and tekke.
- Cross back into [Croatia](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Croatia).
- Stop at [Brela](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Brela%2C%20Croatia) or [Baska Voda](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Baska%20Voda%2C%20Croatia) for a beach walk, not a long beach day. [6]
- Sleep in [Split](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Split%2C%20Croatia) for nightlife and palace atmosphere, or [Trogir](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Trogir%2C%20Croatia) for a quieter old-town base.

Best stops:
- [Blagaj Tekke](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Blagaj%20Tekke%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina) and [Buna spring](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Buna%20Spring%2C%20Blagaj%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina). [1]
- [Brela](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Brela%2C%20Croatia) waterfront, [Brela Stone](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Brela%20Stone%2C%20Brela%2C%20Croatia), and [Punta Rata Beach](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Punta%20Rata%20Beach%2C%20Brela%2C%20Croatia). [6]
- [Split](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Split%2C%20Croatia) evening walk on the [Riva](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Riva%2C%20Split%2C%20Croatia), or [Trogir](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Trogir%2C%20Croatia) if you want easier scale and parking. [2][4]

Food notes:
- On the [Brela](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Brela%2C%20Croatia) / [Baska Voda](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Baska%20Voda%2C%20Croatia) stop, keep food quick unless you are ahead of schedule. [Restaurant Punta Rata](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Restaurant%20Punta%20Rata%2C%20Brela%2C%20Croatia) is the easiest beach-adjacent choice, [Tavern Galinac](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Konoba%20Galinac%2C%20Brela%2C%20Croatia) is better if you want a fuller Dalmatian tavern meal, and [Restaurant Plima](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Restaurant%20Plima%2C%20Brela%2C%20Croatia) is another Brela tourism-listed option. [25]
- If sleeping in [Split](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Split%2C%20Croatia), choose by mood: [Konoba Fetivi](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Konoba%20Fetivi%2C%20Split%2C%20Croatia) for traditional Dalmatian cooking, [K.uzina](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=K.uzina%2C%20Split%2C%20Croatia) for a Michelin Bib Gourmand value meal, [Dvor](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Dvor%2C%20Split%2C%20Croatia) for a splurge with sea-view garden setting, [Buffet Fife](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Buffet%20Fife%2C%20Split%2C%20Croatia) for a busy value-oriented local meal, or [Galija](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Galija%2C%20Split%2C%20Croatia) for casual seafood pizza/gnocchi near the promenade. [15][26][27][28]
- If sleeping in [Trogir](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Trogir%2C%20Croatia), [Franka](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Franka%2C%20Trogir%2C%20Croatia) is the Michelin-inspected pick, while Rick Steves forum users mention [Pizzeria Kristian](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Pizzeria%20Kristian%2C%20Trogir%2C%20Croatia) for informal pasta/seafood and [Don Dino](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Don%20Dino%2C%20Trogir%2C%20Croatia) for a more polished old-town meal. [16][29]

## Day 3: Split / Trogir, Croatia to Zadar, Croatia

Estimated driving: 2 to 2.75 hours total.

Map: [Day 3 route: Split -> Trogir -> Zadar](https://www.google.com/maps/dir/?api=1&origin=Split%2C%20Croatia&destination=Zadar%2C%20Croatia&travelmode=driving&waypoints=Trogir%2C%20Croatia)

- Morning in [Split](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Split%2C%20Croatia): [Diocletian's Palace](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Diocletian%27s%20Palace%2C%20Split%2C%20Croatia), the [Peristyle](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Peristyle%2C%20Split%2C%20Croatia), and [Cathedral of Saint Domnius](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Cathedral%20of%20Saint%20Domnius%2C%20Split%2C%20Croatia). [2][4]
- Stop in [Trogir](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Trogir%2C%20Croatia) if you did not sleep there.
- Continue to [Zadar](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Zadar%2C%20Croatia) for sunset.
- Sleep in [Zadar](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Zadar%2C%20Croatia), preferably on or just outside the old-town peninsula for an easy evening walk to the waterfront.

Best stops:
- [Split](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Split%2C%20Croatia) palace core. [2][4]
- [Trogir](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Trogir%2C%20Croatia) old town. [4]
- [Zadar Sea Organ](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Sea%20Organ%2C%20Zadar%2C%20Croatia) and waterfront sunset. [4]

Food notes:
- Public Rick Steves sources are thin on specific [Zadar](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Zadar%2C%20Croatia) restaurants, so keep the evening flexible after the Sea Organ. For a polished seafood dinner, use [Fosa](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Fosa%2C%20Zadar%2C%20Croatia) or [Kornat](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Kornat%2C%20Zadar%2C%20Croatia); for a more relaxed old-town meal, look at [Pet Bunara](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Pet%20Bunara%2C%20Zadar%2C%20Croatia) or [Bruschetta](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Bruschetta%2C%20Zadar%2C%20Croatia). [4][30][31][32]

Rick Steves-style choice: do [Split](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Split%2C%20Croatia) or [Trogir](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Trogir%2C%20Croatia) well, not both deeply. [1][4]

## Day 4: Zadar, Croatia to Plitvice / Rastoke, Croatia

Estimated driving: 2.25 to 3 hours total.

Map: [Day 4 route: Zadar -> Nin -> Rastoke -> Plitvice Lakes](https://www.google.com/maps/dir/?api=1&origin=Zadar%2C%20Croatia&destination=Plitvice%20Lakes%20National%20Park%2C%20Croatia&travelmode=driving&waypoints=Nin%2C%20Croatia%7CRastoke%2C%20Slunj%2C%20Croatia)

- Morning in [Zadar](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Zadar%2C%20Croatia) old town: [Roman Forum](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Roman%20Forum%2C%20Zadar%2C%20Croatia), [St. Donatus](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Church%20of%20St.%20Donatus%2C%20Zadar%2C%20Croatia) area, waterfront.
- Optional quick stop in [Nin](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Nin%2C%20Croatia).
- Drive inland to [Rastoke](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Rastoke%2C%20Slunj%2C%20Croatia), the waterfall village near [Slunj](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Slunj%2C%20Croatia).
- If you arrive early, use a late-afternoon [Plitvice Lakes](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Plitvice%20Lakes%20National%20Park%2C%20Croatia) ticket for the Lower Lakes. [3][5]
- Sleep near [Plitvice Lakes](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Plitvice%20Lakes%20National%20Park%2C%20Croatia) for the earliest park start, or in [Rastoke](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Rastoke%2C%20Slunj%2C%20Croatia)/[Slunj](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Slunj%2C%20Croatia) for a prettier village setting.

Best stops:
- [Zadar](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Zadar%2C%20Croatia) old town and [Sea Organ](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Sea%20Organ%2C%20Zadar%2C%20Croatia). [4]
- [Nin](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Nin%2C%20Croatia) old town, if you want a short coastal-history stop. [4]
- [Rastoke](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Rastoke%2C%20Slunj%2C%20Croatia) waterfalls and watermills. [3]

Food notes:
- Near [Plitvice Lakes](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Plitvice%20Lakes%20National%20Park%2C%20Croatia), do dinner where you sleep rather than driving around after dark. Rick Steves forum users mention [Hotel Degenija](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Hotel%20Degenija%2C%20Plitvice%20Lakes%2C%20Croatia), [Plitvice Miric Inn](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Plitvice%20Miric%20Inn%2C%20Croatia), and in-park hotels as practical dinner options. If you sleep in [Rastoke](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Rastoke%2C%20Slunj%2C%20Croatia) / [Slunj](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Slunj%2C%20Croatia), ask the lodging host what is open that night; hours can be very seasonal. [17]

## Day 5: Plitvice Lakes, Croatia to Trieste, Italy

Estimated driving: about 3.75 to 4 hours direct, plus any border checks. Keep this as a direct driving day if the 4-hour limit matters.

Map: [Day 5 route: Plitvice Lakes -> Trieste](https://www.google.com/maps/dir/?api=1&origin=Plitvice%20Lakes%20National%20Park%2C%20Croatia&destination=Trieste%2C%20Italy&travelmode=driving)

- Enter [Plitvice Lakes](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Plitvice%20Lakes%20National%20Park%2C%20Croatia) early. [3][5]
- Recommended route in a 5-day trip: choose a shorter park route if you still need to drive to [Trieste](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Trieste%2C%20Italy) the same day. [3][5]
- Drive directly to [Trieste](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Trieste%2C%20Italy).
- Finish and sleep in [Trieste](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Trieste%2C%20Italy), near the old center or waterfront if you want to walk to dinner after returning the car.
- Skip [Opatija](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Opatija%2C%20Croatia), [Piran](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Piran%2C%20Slovenia), [Postojna Cave](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Postojna%20Cave%2C%20Slovenia), and [Skocjan Caves](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Skocjan%20Caves%2C%20Slovenia) unless you add a 6th day. [1][3][5]

Best stops:
- [Plitvice Lakes](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Plitvice%20Lakes%20National%20Park%2C%20Croatia) boardwalks and waterfalls. [3][5]
- [Trieste](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Trieste%2C%20Italy) waterfront and old center after arrival. [1]

Food notes:

## Practical Car Notes

- Confirm the rental car is allowed into [Bosnia and Herzegovina](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina), [Slovenia](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Slovenia), and [Italy](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Italy), and that one-way drop-off in [Trieste](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Trieste%2C%20Italy) is permitted. [9]
- [Bosnia and Herzegovina](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina) is outside Schengen, so expect passport checks entering and leaving BiH. [9]
- [Croatia](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Croatia), [Slovenia](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Slovenia), and [Italy](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Italy) are Schengen countries, but temporary internal border controls may still happen. As of May 17, 2026, the EU lists [Slovenia](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Slovenia) checks on land borders with [Croatia](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Croatia)/[Hungary](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Hungary) through June 21, 2026, and [Italy](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Italy) checks on land borders with [Slovenia](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Slovenia) through June 18, 2026. [8]
- [Slovenia](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Slovenia) requires an e-vignette for motorways and expressways. For most passenger rental cars, this is usually toll class 2A; verify the license plate before buying. [7]
- [Croatia](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Croatia) uses motorway tolls, usually paid at toll booths or by card. [10]
- Do not leave luggage visible in the car at beaches, old towns, or park stops.
- Book timed [Plitvice](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Plitvice%20Lakes%20National%20Park%2C%20Croatia) tickets ahead and sleep near the park before visiting. [5]
